This writing has no affiliation with V for Vendetta the movie.

V for Vendetta is not how our lives are designed to be run but sometimes this kind of mentality subtly finds it's way into our thinking, and acting. The ways of this world are not our ways. The culture of this world is not the way we operate in the kingdom, the two are juxtaposed in everything. The problems occur when we try to import the one into the other, like water and oil, mix them up, shake them up, and in a matter of time you will see the distinction. When we are of the world, we run our own lives, we direct our own lives, and when any kind of decision is to be made consulting our Maker is usually not on the top priority of the to do list. If life had a script, we would be writing it, directing it, and playing all of the leading roles all at the same time and that is not the way we are designed to optimally function. Worst case scenario is that we make V for Vendetta the motto of our lives instead of forgive and give it all to the Lord Jesus. The movie has a noble theme but it is based on the lie of false justice. It is the same lie that the Jews bought into when they demanded to make Jesus a King to crush Rome. The aim was to make the temporal wrongs right but the aim was too low, it was below the target that God had set which was sin and iniquity. The first must be dealt with before the other or else no real progress has been made. True faith is an up and down journey towards perfection. It is not about being perfect but it is all about receiving the perfection of Christ into our lives. There is a difference between a carnal believer with their heart set on the ways of the world and the believer who messes up, sins, or is not fully 100% in the way. Even David had his days when he totally blew it but he is still called a man after God's own heart. Our walk of faith is to be lived out daily, and it is 24/7/365. There are no day's off even if we have an off day. If anyone had the right to live a V lifestyle it was Christ. If anyone had the ability to live a Vendetta, it was Jesus but instead He chose to do and fulfill God's plan, will and purpose. If anyone had a chance to wallow in false justice it was the Lord, He was not worthy of a single injustice He suffered and yet He still willfully went through them on our behalf. In Christ the script has changed, the desire to be in control is gone because He is able to lead and guide us by His word and Spirit much better than we can. The enemy has a script and it is to steal, kill, destroy, and fill you with fear, negativity, and condemnation, and the longer we insist to run our own lives, the more the enemy will try to keep injecting his script into our lives. When we enroll as soldiers of the cross, we get orders from heaven and our Commander in Chief provides for His own battles He commissions us for.
